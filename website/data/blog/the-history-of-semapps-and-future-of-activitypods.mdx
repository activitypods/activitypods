_ActivityPods was built on SemApps, a toolkit for developing semantic web applications that dates back to the early 2010s. The technological choices that have guided and continue to guide ActivityPods' development are intimately linked to SemApps and its philosophy._

SemApps (for Semantic Applications) was conceived within the Assemblée Virtuelle, an association founded in 2011 in France, which was initially conceived as a contributory citizen think-tank.

Its first project, Idées-Mix, had the ambition of valorizing ideas proposed by stakeholders, and transforming them into concrete projects by putting them in touch with other stakeholders and resources. But the small initial team soon came up against technical difficulties.

Following the discovery of the semantic web and, above all, a meeting in 2013 with Henry Story (Solid's historic contributor, who sadly passed away this autumn), the association's purpose was reoriented towards the creation of collaborative, decentralized software solutions.

At the time, SemApps was not a toolbox, but an application to help organizations cooperate by opening up their data for reading and writing. v1 was essentially developed by Sébastien Lemoine and Romain Weeger, based on Semantic Forms, a software package developed by another member of the Virtual Assembly, Jean-Marc Vanel.

The first instance of SemApps was deployed for a gigantic ephemeral third-place in Paris: Les Grands Voisins. It made visible the myriad organizations and individuals occupying the space, and helped them to cooperate more effectively. It was a great cooperative database.

![The first instance of SemApps for Les Grands Voisins](../../src/assets/images/blog/grands-voisins.png)

<ImageCaption>The first instance of SemApps for Les Grands Voisins</ImageCaption>

Of course, the data was stored in semantic form. There were also features specific to the semantic web, such as the ability to import one's profile from another instance, or to add links between resources of distant instances. The Assemblée Virtuelle association also had its own instance.

Unfortunately, following Sébastien Lemoine's departure for other adventures, development of the project came to a halt. The bodies were maintained for a few years, but at some point they ceased to function and no one was able to revive them. This coincided with the closure of Les Grands Voisins.
